Language List

Finnish

Finnish is the primary language of Finland and is spoken natively by about five million people. The country is known for its cold weather, happy people, sauna culture, and the northern lights. The Finns' tongue is known for being grammar dense and complex to non-native speakers.


Korean

Korean is the primary language of North and South Korea and is spoken by nearly seventy-five to eighty million people. South Korea, the more traveled country of the two is known for its recent innovation and economic success, its unique food culture, K-pop music, and more. The Korean language is sometimes said to have one of the most intuitive and unique writing systems, Hangeul, that is now used in modern language.


Spanish

Spanish is the primary language of many nations worldwide, but originated from the European nation of Spain. There are nearly five hundred native speakers globally. This language is attached to many cultures including Latin American, European, and African cultures. It is part of the Romance Language family, and is said to be one of the more manageable langauges for English speakers to pick up.


French

French is the primary language of many countries worldwide as well. Similar to Spanish, this language can be found in Africa, Europe, North America, and some Pacific islands, and is spoken natively by around eighty million people worldwide. This langauge is also a member of the Romance Language Family, though its pronunciation and spelling may prove to be more difficult to English speakers than Spanish.


Polish

Polish is the native tongue of Poland and there are about forty million native speakers. Poland is known for its beautiful landscapes, stunning architecture, and delicious food. This language is known for its consonant clusters, unique alphabet, and complex grammar. Learning this language is not for the faint of heart.
